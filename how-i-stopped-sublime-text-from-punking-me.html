How I Stopped Sublime Text from Punking Me

Posted on Fri 03 April 2015 in Text Editor

I use “command+enter” in Sublime Text to submit R code to the terminal so much that the right thumb, ring-finger movement is engrained in my muscle memory like the euphonium part to Festive Overture. This functionality works thanks to the line-by-line plug-in developed by Alyssa Frazee and related Enhanced-R plug-in by Randy Lai.

One day, out of the blue, “command+enter” broke. Instead of sending the line or highlighted selection to the most recently active terminal window, “command+enter” would send it to the R GUI, opening it if it wasn’t already. I spent hours on my office iMac trying to figure out why and eventually fixed the problem without understanding how I fixed it and without documenting any of the steps. Then, a few days later, my advisor had the same issue. We fixed the problem on his computer with a similar undocumented series of hacks. (Mainly restoring old backup files.) Today, it broke on my laptop. Fool me once, shame on you, Sublime; fool me twice (and three times) shame on me!

The fix was simple. In /Library/Application Support/Sublime Text 2/Packages/R-Box there should be a file called R-box.sublime-settings and the line “App” : “Terminal” should be in there.
