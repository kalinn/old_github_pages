About

I am a post-doctoral researcher in the PennSIVE lab at the University of Pennsylvania. Broadly, my research is focused on leveraging tools from statistics, causal inference, and machine learning to address confounding in observational neuroimaging data.

I am currently on leave from Penn to attend the Fall 1 batch of the Recurse Center.

I earned my Ph.D. in Statistics from North Carolina State University in May 2014. My dissertation comprised methods for estimating optimal dynamic treatment regimes that target non-mean summaries (e.g., quantiles) of a patient outcome distribution. In addition, I developed a framework for estimating dynamic treatment regimes that balance tradeoffs between competing outcomes such as drug efficacy and side effect burden.

Before persuing a career in statistics, I graduated from the University of Michigan with a degree in Euphonium Performance.
